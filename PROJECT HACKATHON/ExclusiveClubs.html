NITK NON-EXCLUSIVE CLUBS

EXCLUSIVE CLUBS

Effectively there are 6 Exclusive Clubs in NITK. While all of them are not open to recruitments for First Years, all of them conduct events for First Years and some of them like IEEE have a Student Membership Drive for First Years. As you become NITKian , all these clubs welcome you with their own induction programs too where you get to know what NITK offers you apart from academics. The club induction program includes information about the club along with some fun events.After attending the induction programs which tells you a lot about college, you get to know that you made a good choice choosing NITK. Initial days of college are heart warming as each day you get to know more. All clubs organize different events which include making doodles, debates ,presentations, quizzes, creative writing, video making, making business plans, gaming and many more.Participating in these events help you to get to know your batch-mates and seniors. Moreover it also tells you about your interest. All the events are filled with enjoyment , make sure that you participate in most of them:) There are various SMPs for core, non-core as well as hobbies. In SMPs you are mentored by your Seniors , so you can learn whatever you wish to learn in a very systematic and friendly environment. Moreover you can enroll in as many SMP you want, your participation and performance also gives you an edge to get into the exclusive club you want to be in. Some SMPs also involve doing projects with your seniors which is definitely beneficial in short and long terms. Details about the SMPs can be found on the club websites. Summers are a good time to brush up your skills so do enroll in the SMPs , taking the most out of your college:

1)IEEE (DO CLICK HERE to access IEEE-NITK VIDEO for more insights)

IEEE stands for the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers. It is the world's largest professional association advancing innovation and technological excellence for the benefit of humanity. Most people would look at the name and think, “I’m not in ECE or EEE, what is the point of joining IEEE?” This is a completely false notion. IEEE-NITK has three main SIGs(Special Interest Groups) which cater to students of all branches of engineering. So even if you are in Chemical or CS, IEEE has something for you. After all, what’s in a name? The three SIGs of IEEE are:

a)CompSoc, is a conglomeration of students with a passion for Computer Science and Information Technology. b)Piston, is a special interest group dedicated to the engineering applications of Mechanical, Automation, Civil, Chemical and Material Sciences. c)Diode, is a team of students with a strong interest in the field of Electronics and Electrical Science.

In general, non-circuit branches (Mech, Chemical, Meta, Mining, Civil) come under Piston, IT/CSE under Python and ECE/EEE under Diode. However, you can join whichever SIG you want based on your interest. It is not dependent on your branch. IEEE regularly hosts workshops for the students where they can learn about things like Python, JavaScript, etc (Python SIG), CATIA (Piston SIG) and Op-Amps (Diode SIG). In addition they host various events and games throughout the year which are quite fun to attend.

2)ACM(DO CLICK HERE to access ACM-NITK VIDEO for more insights)

ACM NITK Chapter is a perfect assortment of a variety of skill sets and interest groups – both technical and non-technical. The club consists of five special interest groups (SIGs) or ‘Yantras’ (‘cause we’re fancy that way)-

1. Vidyut: Electrical and Electronics 2. Saahitya: Literature and Fine Arts 3. Yantrika: For all things core 4. Kaaryavarta: Entrepreneurship and Business 5. Sanganitra: Computer Science

ACM also has 2 auxiliary SIGs: The social-initiatives group Krutagnata carries out activities like Shiksha and Vidhyarthi to educate the students of the NITK Kannada Medium School. ACM-W lives by its motto to inform, involve and inspire more and more women to take up engineering and technology. Quashing all myths and prejudices, they conduct events- flagship event being Resurgence- to instill confidence and high ambition in women.

Members have the freedom to join and work with any or all of the Yantras depending on their interests and commitment. These SIGs regularly hold Knowledge Exchange Programs (KEPs) to introduce beginner-level concepts from these domains. ACM hosts multiple events throughout the year, their flagship event being Innovision, a week-long event with all the SIGs conducting specific competitions.

3)ROTARACT CLUB(DO CLICK HERE to access ROTARACT CLUB VIDEO for more insights)

The Rotaract Club, NITK Chapter, is a social welfare club affiliated to its international parent: Rotary. With a plethora of initiatives and social impact projects in place, they seek to aid the underprivileged in times of need, while focusing on self development and matters such as animal welfare.

The club hosts multiple interdisciplinary initiatives, the significant ones being:

  • Rotech - Utilizing the technical competence of members for societal welfare oriented projects.
  • Humans of Service - Connecting with highly reputed individuals working in the field of societal impact and sharing their story.
  • Rotalks - Spreading awareness concerning critical issues via video interactions.
  • Mental Health Awareness Week, which stands out as a beautiful reminder to address our mental health and place importance in self care.

Other events include orphanage trips, the Karavali Marathon and the Roto Play, where money collected for the play is donated to charity, etc. The Rotaract club believes in a world where people unite and take action to create lasting change — across the globe, in our communities, and in ourselves.

4)IE(DO CLICK HERE to access IE CLUB VIDEO for more insights)

One of the oldest professional societies on campus the institution of Engineers or IE was established in 1969. It is a student organisation affiliated to the Institution of Engineers, India. The club's primary focus is to help members develop diverse skills.IE also has five auxiliary SIGs:

  • Script
  • Robotics
  • Capital
  • Tectonic
  • Vriddhi (Social initiatives)

Members have the freedom to join and work with any or all of the SIGs depending on their interests and commitment. These SIGs regularly hold Knowledge Exchange Programs (KEPs) to introduce beginner-level concepts from these domains. Moreover, there are internal hackathons and Group Discussion sessions.

IE hosts multiple events throughout the year, flagship event includes:

  • KOTH (the whole of IE conducts events)
  • Enigma (they are conducted by each SIG)
  • Samwad (Interaction with alumni for technical topics)
  • Spark (TEDx series of IE) and many more.

5)ISTE(DO CLICK HERE to access ISTE VIDEO for more insights)

The Indian Society for Technical Education also known as ISTE is a nationally recognized professional nonprofit making society which contributes in various missions of the union government.It is the only national organisation of educators in the field of engineering and technology. In our college ISTE organizes some of the best events for the students, most of which are not very technical.

One of their most renowned events for which nearly 85% of the freshers participate every year is Scotland Yard! It's a function as they describe it. Be sure not to miss it!

ISTE is also one of the few clubs to conduct some really useful workshops especially for the first years which include Arduino, AutoCAD and Aeronautics. The festival of technology that they conduct is called Transcend and is a phenomenon every year.

6)IET(DO CLICK HERE to access IET CLUB VIDEO for more insights)

The Institute of Engineering Technology (IET) is affiliated to IET London, which is one of the world’s leading professional societies for the engineering and technology community . They conduct workshops and seminars throughout the academic calendar striving to impact relevant skills to the students. Apart from this, they strive to invite eminent personalities to conduct lectures for the benefit of the club members and keeping doors opened for others interested.

IET NITK consists of five different Special Interest Groups:

  • Cipher SIG, brings together people interested in Computer Science and Information Technology.
  • Rovisp SIG, brings together people who are passionate about Electronics and Electrical Technology.
  • Torsion SIG, brings together people interested in Mechanical, Civil, Chemical and Material Sciences.
  • Inkheart SIG, is the new Lit&Phil for people who are passionate about literature, music etc.
  • Medium SIG, is dedicated to people interested in Media and Marketing.
